1 | Narrator | Thought | Thunder rumbled off in the distance, yet there was no sign of a storm. Clear blue skies and a light breeze, the only thing out of place was a massive pit surrounded by geometric markings on the ground. It reminded me of what we flew out of in the desert. We had to be miles and miles away, to see it so completely, even this high up. Grazing animals were like ants as they meandered between their several groups around the gaping abyss. Oblivious to the rising ground I impacted and deflected down the sloped hills. Skimming the tops of the green and brown blades, I braced for the short fall as I disengaged the Decoherence Mechanism. Sliding for a few meters produced grass stains on my elbows and knees. As I got to my feet Kai and the Emissaries silently touched down around me, Kai holding back a laugh after watching me hit the ground. Gazing into the valley, the uncultivated expanse within something so artifical entranced us; questions shouldered their way into awareness. Left alone, life will do as it always does and settle into a niche. Whether it currently exists or not is irrelevant, Equilibrium is sought out, and it will be found. Energy, will always be conserved. Life evolves as a response to its environment, so how do they fit into this?
